The history of India is marked by regional kingdoms and cultural diversity, particularly during the early medieval age from 600 to 1200 CE. This period saw the rise of various dynasties, including the Chalukyas, Pallavas, Pandyas, and Cholas, each controlling their respective regions. No single ruler was able to create a unified empire, and the caste system began to show regional differences. During this time, there was a resurgence of Hinduism, and the development of modern languages of the subcontinent. The creation of devotional hymns in the Tamil language in the 6th and 7th centuries had a significant impact, leading to the growth of temple towns and urbanization. Indian culture and political systems were also exported to Southeast Asia, where they had a lasting influence. The arrival of Muslim Central Asian nomadic clans in the 10th century led to the establishment of the Islamic Delhi Sultanate in 1206, which controlled much of North India and made forays into South India. Although the sultanate was initially disruptive, it ultimately allowed its non-Muslim subject population to maintain their own laws and customs. The sultanate also played a significant role in protecting India from Mongol raiders in the 13th century, setting the stage for centuries of migration and cultural exchange.
